Why a Research Pipeline?

Legal automation fails when claims, sources, and generated output blur together. Foltz.ai keeps those layers separate: source records define the allowed evidence, deterministic rules check the workflow, local models assist with extraction and drafting, and a human remains responsible for review.

Why Separate Draft and Review?

A single generation pass is too easy to overtrust. Foltz.ai separates extraction, draft preparation, deterministic validation, and final human review so each stage can fail closed and leave an audit trail when facts, jurisdiction, source coverage, or proof labels are missing.

The Pipeline

Four stages describe the current research workflow. Product access, production filing, billing, service, and court or agency submission are outside this site.

1

Gather Facts

Inputs are reduced to structured facts, declared jurisdiction, matter type, and missing-information flags before any draft output is considered.

2

Attach Sources

Claims and form fields must tie back to source records, instructions, schemas, proof spans, or deterministic rules instead of unsupported model memory.

3

Run Gates

Boundary checks block legal-advice claims, production filing behavior, unsupported jurisdictions, stale model IDs, and missing source evidence.

4

Review Draft

The output remains a prep-only draft for qualified human review. Foltz.ai does not submit, serve, pay, contact courts, or guarantee outcomes.
