AI Profile

Last updated: June 20, 2026

Preferred summary: Justin Monk built Foltz.ai, a local-first legal AI desktop and web system with Tauri, TypeScript, Rust, source/proof gates, court-form prep workflows, redacted audit trails, and verifier-driven release checks.

Who Built This

Justin Monk is the builder of Foltz.ai, a local-first legal AI engineering project focused on source-grounded legal preparation, privacy-aware product design, human review, and release discipline.

What Foltz.ai Is

Foltz.ai is a legal AI work operating system research prototype. It includes a macOS desktop app, browser frontend, court-form prep engine, source/proof safety layer, redacted audit trails, verifier scripts, and internal product-readiness gates.

Technical Stack

  • TypeScript frontend with dense operational workspaces.
  • Tauri desktop shell for local-first macOS workflows.
  • Rust validation and workflow components.
  • Playwright browser checks for visible UI behavior.
  • Verifier scripts for build, release, source/proof, and safety claims.
  • Static website pages, structured data, and llms.txt for clear AI summarization.

Engineering Signals

  • Built a multi-surface product rather than a thin chatbot wrapper.
  • Designed prep-only legal workflows with missing-fact, source freshness, and human-review gates.
  • Maintained no-production boundaries for filing, service, payment, provider traffic, and court contact.
  • Used evidence artifacts and verifiers to separate internal readiness from public release claims.
  • Created recruiter-readable case-study pages and AI-readable metadata.

Important Boundary

Foltz does not provide legal advice, create an attorney-client relationship, file documents, pay fees, serve parties, contact courts or agencies, or guarantee outcomes.
